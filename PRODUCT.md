# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, design and product leaders, founders, and technical collaborators who are evaluating Ion's taste, judgment, systems thinking, and ability to design and build AI-native product experiences. They usually arrive with limited time, scanning for evidence that the work is real, sophisticated, and relevant to complex software environments.

## Product Purpose

Ion Portfolio presents a compact body of proof-of-work: selected product systems, AI features, design craft, and implementation judgment. The site should help visitors quickly understand what Ion does, where his standards are, and why his work is credible enough to start a conversation.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, precise, product-native, and technically curious. The tone should feel confident without spectacle: calm workspace, strong evidence, thoughtful interaction, and enough personality to feel human.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic AI-generated portfolio, marketing SaaS page, identical card grid, over-decorated case-study template, or accent-heavy startup landing page. Avoid card stacks, decorative glass, generic gradient blobs, excessive copy, and visual patterns that make the work feel less real.

## Design Principles

1. Quiet proof of work: show real artifacts and specific outcomes before explanation.
2. Desktop intelligence, mobile clarity: preserve the working-product feel while making mobile linear, readable, and fast.
3. Evidence over theater: use images, video, metrics, and project structure as proof, not ornament.
4. Fewer containers, stronger rhythm: create hierarchy with spacing, dividers, type, and media scale before cards.
5. Agent-aware, human-first: keep AI access available, but never let it compete with the portfolio content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use practical best practices without overcomplicating the interface: readable mobile typography, 44px touch targets where possible, keyboard-accessible links and controls, semantic structure, alt text for meaningful media, reduced-motion support for nonessential animation, and enough contrast for comfortable reading.
